Ancient Survivors

Crocodiles have lived on Earth for over 200 million years! They survived the extinction that killed the dinosaurs and are often called "living fossils."

Impressive Size

The largest crocodiles can grow up to 20 feet long and weigh over 2,000 pounds. That's bigger than most cars!

Perfect Predators

Crocodiles are semi-aquatic reptiles with powerful jaws, armor-like skin, and can hold their breath underwater for up to an hour.

Global Species

There are 23 different species of crocodiles living in tropical regions around the world, from tiny dwarf crocodiles to massive saltwater crocodiles.

Swamps & Marshes

Crocodiles love freshwater swamps where they can hide among plants and ambush prey. The murky water helps them stay camouflaged.

Rivers & Lakes

Many crocodiles live in slow-moving rivers and large lakes. They prefer areas with shallow banks where they can sun themselves.

Coastal Areas

Saltwater crocodiles can live in ocean waters and estuaries. They're excellent swimmers and can travel long distances in the sea.

Tropical Climates

Crocodiles need warm temperatures year-round. You'll find them in Africa, Australia, Asia, and the Americas - but only in hot, tropical regions.

Where in the World?

Crocodiles live on every continent except Europe and Antarctica!

  • Australia: Saltwater and freshwater crocodiles
  • Africa: Nile crocodiles in rivers and lakes
  • Americas: American crocodiles and alligators
  • Asia: Saltwater crocodiles and many smaller species

What Do Crocodiles Eat?

You're looking at a apex predator in action! Crocodiles have perfected the art of hunting over millions of years.

That death roll technique isn't just for show - it helps them tear off chunks of meat and disorient their prey. Physics meets prehistoric power!

Crocodiles have a four-chambered stomach like cows! They can digest bones, horns, and hooves thanks to super strong stomach acid.

Here's wild: They can survive up to one year without eating by slowing their metabolism. Try doing that on your diet!

Fish & Aquatic Animals

Fish make up most of a crocodile's diet. They also eat frogs, turtles, and water birds that come to drink at the water's edge.

Large Mammals

Big crocodiles can take down deer, wild boar, and even buffalo! They use their powerful jaws and death roll technique.

Birds & Reptiles

Crocodiles are opportunistic hunters. They'll eat water birds, snakes, and even other crocodiles if they get the chance.

Patient Hunters

Crocodiles can wait hours without moving, then explode into action when prey comes close. They're ambush predators!

Hunting Facts

  • Crocodiles can go months without eating if needed
  • They swallow stones to help digest food in their stomachs
  • Baby crocodiles eat insects, small fish, and crustaceans
  • Their bite force is stronger than a T-Rex!
